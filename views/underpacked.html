Underpacked

Underpacked! – Master Degree Project

Underpacked! is a third-person cooperative game, free-to-play, developed by the Spanish studio Drunken Duck Co. in collaboration with Voxel School and Padaone Games. With a vibrant cartoon aesthetic and satirical humor, the game blends time management, chaotic action, intergalactic deliveries, and a playful critique of modern work culture.

Currently available on Steam, it offers single-player, local co-op, Remote Play Together, and split-screen PvP modes. Players take on the role of overworked space couriers, struggling with run-down ships, impossible deadlines, and threats like space pirates — all while trying to deliver packages on time.

The official demo launched on July 11, 2025, featuring four frantic levels and the option to play solo (with the AI companion Jul.IA) or in local co-op. Key gameplay mechanics include:

  • Customizing packages with specific colors before delivery.
  • Defending the ship using a turret with multiple ammo types.
  • Managing and repairing ship systems under constant pressure.
  • Launching deliveries through the space mailbox before the time limit.

The demo received an overwhelmingly positive reception, achieving 100% positive reviews on Steam for its visual style, fast pace, and humor.

My Contribution to the Project

I worked across a wide range of areas, from gameplay mechanics to technical optimization. Some of my key contributions include:

  • Gameplay Mechanics:
    • Designed and implemented the turret system with multiple ammo types, each with unique effects.
    • Developed interactive ship systems such as hull breaches, mailbox movement, and respawn capsule logic.
  • Visuals & Animation:
    • Created and integrated the player and bot animation systems.
    • Contextual animations for repairing, shooting, and carrying packages.
  • Technical Art:
    • Dynamic tube and cable generators for ship interiors.
    • Implemented holographic displays and in-world UI elements.
  • Visual Effects (VFX):
    • Explosions, shooting effects, smoke, fire, electricity, and asteroid visuals.
  • Post-processing & Audio:
    • Outline effects, hit flashes, and color grading for gameplay readability.
    • Integrated FMOD for all sound effects and dynamic music layers.
  • Optimization & QA:
    • Used Unreal profiler and memory debugger to maintain stable performance.
    • Testing, bug fixing, and continuous playtesting.

What I Learned

Throughout development, I improved my teamwork, project management, Unreal Engine technical skills, audio integration, QA processes, version control with Perforce, and automated builds with Jenkins.
